Justin Purnell is the founder of Ledge Partners, a strategic advisory firm helping growth-stage companies translate complex strategy into measurable results. His career spans two decades across Wall Street, media, and e-commerce, blending analytical rigor with creative problem-solving.

Justin began his career at Goldman Sachs, where he spent seven years as a high-yield credit research analyst. He published over 400 pieces of institutional research covering the Energy, Gaming, Lodging, and Leisure sectors, advising portfolio managers on credit risk and investment opportunities across a multi-billion-dollar coverage universe.

After earning his MBA from the Tuck School of Business at Dartmouth, Justin moved into digital media. At UCB Comedy (the Upright Citizens Brigade), he launched UCBComedy.com and built the organization's digital revenue streams. He went on to serve as Director of Content Strategy and Operations at NBCUniversal, where he helped launch Seeso, NBCU's comedy-focused subscription video-on-demand service. He also directed Amy Poehler's web series "Smart Girls at the Party," an ON Networks production aimed at building self-esteem in early adolescent girls.

As Vice President of Strategy and Business Development for NBCUniversal Digital Enterprises, Justin shaped digital commerce and content strategy across the organization's portfolio. He later joined Hotels at Home as Head of Product, where he rebuilt the company's digital platform across 70+ branded e-commerce storefronts for Marriott International — including ShopMarriott.com, ShopWestin.com, and ShopRitz-Carlton.com — driving multimillion-dollar revenue growth and leading a cross-functional team of engineers, designers, and merchandisers.

Justin is an active Swift and Python developer. His open-source projects include BusinessMath, a financial computation framework, and he contributes to projects in AI/ML, data science, and static site generation. He is also a skilled improvisational comedian, having trained and performed at the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre in New York.

A 2000 graduate of Princeton University with an A.B. in Economics (certificates in Finance and Political Philosophy), Justin currently serves as President of the Princeton Class of 2000 and sits on the class Executive Committee. He is Co-President of the Tuck Club of New York, has been Secretary of St. Albans' Great(est?) Class of 1996 since graduation, is an original Headmaster's Counselor, and co-leads the school's Black Alumni Alliance.

Justin is based in the New York metropolitan area.
